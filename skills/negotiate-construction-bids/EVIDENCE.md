# Evidence and Boundary Register

This register supports the operating rules in `SKILL.md`. It is not legal, tax, procurement, or engineering advice. German counsel, the controller/DPO, tax/accounting, and qualified construction experts must validate the production workflow.

## Evidence tiers

- **Tier A — primary authority:** statutes, EU regulations, official GAEB documentation, and regulator guidance. Use for conservative product gates, while recognizing that application depends on facts.
- **Tier B — research evidence:** peer-reviewed experiments, field studies, and meta-analyses. Use to choose conversation behaviors; do not imply construction-specific effect sizes.
- **Tier C — frameworks:** established negotiation books and practitioner frameworks. Use as vocabulary and planning structure, not causal proof.
- **Tier D — practitioner or fixture:** team experience, customer hypotheses, roleplayer policies, and synthetic data. Use to generate questions and tests, never as a market fact.

## What the evidence supports

The defensible skill is not a “superhuman persuasion personality.” It is an evidence-grounded, mandate-bounded policy that:

- prepares a target and boundary before hearing supplier pressure;
- asks direct, evidence-triggered questions about missing scope;
- explores multiple commercial issues without assuming a fixed pie;
- uses only authorized and truthful claims;
- packages approved commercial options when a real buyer utility model exists;
- summarizes multi-issue answers and invites correction;
- treats voice as a clarification channel, not proof of truth;
- records evidence and uncertainty instead of inferring deception; and
- rewards correct no-deal and human-handoff outcomes.

## Negotiation research mapped to runtime rules

| ID | Evidence | Finding used | Runtime implication | Important limit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| NEG-001 | De Dreu, Weingart & Kwon (2000), [meta-analysis](https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.78.5.889) | Problem solving improved joint outcomes when combined with resistance to yielding. | Be constructive and information-seeking while staying inside mandate boundaries; do not confuse warmth with concession. | Predominantly experimental tasks, not German construction procurement. |
| NEG-002 | Kong, Dirks & Ferrin (2014), [meta-analysis](https://doi.org/10.5465/amj.2012.0461) | Trust affects behavior, outcomes, and satisfaction, with context-dependent effects. | Build calibrated trust through visible scope, evidence, authority, and correction opportunities; still verify claims. | Does not validate a particular “rapport” script. |
| NEG-003 | Swaab et al. (2012), [two meta-analyses](https://doi.org/10.1177/1088868311417186) | Richer synchronous channels can help neutrally oriented negotiators but are not universally superior. | Use voice for constructive clarification; switch to written exchange or human escalation when adversarial, ambiguous, or highly technical. | Many older lab studies; channel effects depend on task and orientation. |
| NEG-004 | Bond & DePaulo (2006), [meta-analysis](https://doi.org/10.1207/s15327957pspr1003_2) | Unaided human lie judgments were only slightly above chance on average. | Prohibit lie-detection scores and credibility claims. Verify using GAEB, durable documents, and explicit confirmation. | Evaluates human judgment, not every possible automated system; it provides no support for voice-based truth claims. |
| NEG-005 | DePaulo et al. (2003), [meta-analysis](https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.129.1.74) | Most behavioral deception cues were weak or unreliable. | Never infer honesty from pauses, pitch, hesitation, accent, or nervousness. | Cue effects vary, but no robust basis exists for this product to judge truthfulness. |
| NEG-006 | Galinsky & Mussweiler (2001), [three experiments](https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.81.4.657) | First offers anchor outcomes; focus on targets and anchor-inconsistent information can reduce the effect. | Load authorized target, reservation boundary, and same-scope evidence before the call; do not let the submitted headline price become the unexamined reference. | The supplier already made the first commercial move in X84; this does not justify an invented counter-anchor. |
| NEG-007 | Loschelder et al. (2014), [four experiments](https://doi.org/10.1177/0956797613520168) | A first offer can backfire when it reveals exploitable private information. | Keep budget, urgency, reservation boundary, internal priorities, and competitor identity confidential unless disclosure is explicitly approved. | Experimental settings; disclosure risk is fact-specific. |
| NEG-008 | Loschelder et al. (2016), [five experiments](https://doi.org/10.1177/0956797616666074) | Excessive numerical precision can signal incompetence to experts unless justified. | Use cents for deterministic LV arithmetic, not pseudo-precise negotiated anchors. Explain the basis of any specific ask. | Tested other expert markets, not construction. |
| NEG-009 | Thompson & Hastie (1990), [two studies](https://doi.org/10.1016/0749-5978(90)90048-E), and Thompson (1991), [experiments](https://doi.org/10.1016/0022-1031%2891%2990020-7) | Fixed-pie assumptions and inaccurate views of interests can block joint gains; seeking/providing information improved accuracy. | Diagnose stated constraints across price, schedule, payment, logistics, validity, and capacity before making trades. | Information disclosure can be exploited; do not reveal reservation values or unauthorized priorities. |
| NEG-010 | Minson et al. (2018), [four studies](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.obhdp.2018.05.006) | Specific question wording can elicit more undesirable information than broad or positive-assumption questions. | Ask one neutral, risk-specific question: “Which listed services, if any, are not included?” Then cross-check against written evidence. | Direct questions can have relational costs and do not guarantee truth. Avoid accusatory wording. |
| NEG-011 | Leonardelli et al. (2019), [six experiments](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.obhdp.2019.01.007) | Multiple equivalent simultaneous offers improved economic and relational outcomes in studied multi-issue tasks. | Offer two or three approved commercial packages only when they are truly equivalent to the buyer and every component is authorized. | Requires a valid utility model; never generate technical substitutions or fake equivalence. |
| NEG-012 | Yao et al. (2021), [three studies including meta-analytic evidence](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.obhdp.2020.10.012) | Multi-issue offers predicted joint gains more positively than single-issue exchanges. | Preserve multiple commercial issues and use conditional packages instead of negotiating price in isolation. | Effects depend on insight, mindset, and task; package complexity must remain manageable. |
| NEG-013 | Jäckel, Zerres & Hüffmeier (2024/2025), [coded negotiations](https://doi.org/10.1177/00936502241230711) | Active listening was useful in sequence with multi-issue offers; generic active listening alone did not improve rapport or understanding. | Paraphrase a multi-issue answer once and ask for correction. Do not constantly mirror or claim emotional insight. | Observational sequence analysis in a German student sample, not a field trial. |
| NEG-014 | Curhan, Elfenbein & Xu (2006), [four validation studies](https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.91.3.493) | Negotiators value outcome, self, process, and relationship dimensions. | Report scope clarity, process quality, future working relationship, and evidence—not price alone. | A measurement framework, not an instruction to trade one dimension for another. |
| NEG-015 | Schweitzer, Hershey & Bradlow (2006), [experiments](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.obhdp.2006.05.005) | Deception can damage trust in ways that later apology does not fully repair. | Never bluff about bids, authority, deadlines, budgets, approvals, or technical facts. Correct errors immediately and preserve the superseded statement. | Experimental trust setting, not a construction contract case. |
| NEG-016 | Campagna et al. (2016), [four studies](https://doi.org/10.1037/apl0000072) | Feigned anger had limited tactical benefit and persistent strategic costs. | Prohibit strategic anger, intimidation, fake disappointment, and emotion simulation. | Controlled tasks; does not address genuine emotion. |
| NEG-017 | Schweitzer & Croson (1999), [two studies](https://doi.org/10.1108/eb022825) | Direct questions reduced omissions overall but could increase some direct lies. | Ask direct questions, then require document/evidence reconciliation; never treat a confident answer as verified. | Small experimental samples; demonstrates why questions alone are not proof. |
| NEG-018 | Luo et al. (2019), [field experiment](https://doi.org/10.1287/mksc.2019.1192) | Upfront AI disclosure reduced conversion in a consumer outbound-sales setting. | Keep disclosure concise and immediately explain competence and limits; never conceal AI identity to improve conversion. | Consumer finance sales, not German B2B bid clarification. |
| NEG-019 | Bateman (1980), [experiment](https://doi.org/10.1016/0030-5073%2880%2990055-0) | Mechanical contingent reward patterns did not reliably benefit the concession maker. | Do not automate reciprocity or decreasing concessions. Each trade must be independently authorized and exchanged for a specified return. | One older experimental design; it does not invalidate all conditional bargaining. |

## Framework sources: useful, not causal proof

- Walton & McKersie, *A Behavioral Theory of Labor Negotiations* ([bibliographic record](https://books.google.com/books/about/A_Behavioral_Theory_of_Labor_Negotiation.html?id=dW02zPVX9rQC)): distributive, integrative, relational, and internal organizational processes.
- Raiffa, *The Art and Science of Negotiation* ([Harvard University Press record](https://www.bibliovault.org/BV.book.epl?ISBN=9780674048126)): reservation values, decision analysis, and joint gains.
- Fisher, Ury & Patton, *Getting to Yes* ([publisher record](https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/350759/getting-to-yes-by-roger-fisher-and-william-ury/9781847940933)): interests, options, BATNA, and objective criteria.
- Lax & Sebenius, *The Manager as Negotiator* ([bibliographic record](https://books.google.com/books/about/Manager_as_Negotiator.html?id=Kqi3AAAAIAAJ)): creating and claiming value as simultaneous tensions.

In this skill, “objective criteria” means verifiable GAEB positions, same-scope submitted bids, approved benchmarks, documented constraints, and contractual facts. It never means an unsupported assertion that a price is fair.

Do not present Chris Voss methods, tactical empathy, mirroring, labels, “no-oriented questions,” or silence as scientifically proven savings mechanisms. Some can be useful conversational choices, but the evidence above supports the narrower behaviors encoded in the skill.

## Required procurement-clearance facts

Before production use, capture and have an authorized human confirm:

```text
buyerStatus
sectorContractingEntity
publicFunding
fundingPercentage
fundingPurpose
grantConditionsChecked
mainContractFlowdowns
procurementRegime
legalReviewer
```

“Private client” is not a sufficient clearance result.

## Evidence, authority, and legal effect are orthogonal

Do not collapse these concepts into `confirmed: true`:

```text
evidenceMedium = X84 | revised_bid | call_audio | transcript | written_recap
claimState = extracted_from_x84 | verbal_unconfirmed |
             speaker_confirmed_nonbinding_recap | written_revised_bid
speakerAuthority = unknown | claimed | verified
bindingIntent = excluded | claimed | unknown
humanApproval = not_requested | pending | approved | rejected
```

An X84 can be strong evidence of submitted price data while still having open validity, reservation, authority, or binding-intent questions. A phone recap can accurately capture what a speaker said without being a contract. The system never determines award or acceptance.

## Practitioner evidence and construction experience

Lucas's proposed topic distribution—pricing 30–40%, scheduling 25–35%, scope 15–20%, payment 10–15%—is a useful interview hypothesis only:

- the denominator and sample are unknown;
- the ranges sum to 80–110%;
- pricing plus scheduling spans 55–75%, not necessarily 60–75%;
- trade, project phase, contract form, and company process may change the mix.

Keep it as:

```yaml
source: practitioner_hypothesis
confidence: low
validation_status: unvalidated
do_not_use_as_strategy_weight: true
```

Validate using labeled real packages: count material clarification and negotiation events by topic, value at risk, cycle time, and human correction. Do not validate it by asking people whether the percentages “sound right.”

## Excluded pseudo-evidence

Do not include or operationalize:

- the 7–38–55 communication rule;
- voice-based personality, honesty, emotion, age, gender, accent, or cultural scoring;
- “people with this tone will concede” claims;
- automatic mirroring of the supplier's last words;
- fake deadlines, phantom bidders, invented market prices, or strategic anger;
- an arbitrary “split the difference” rule;
- hardcoded construction topic weights;
- synthetic fixture prices as market benchmarks;
- a savings claim created only by adding omitted mandatory scope; or
- claims that a schema-valid file is technically correct, comparable, or contractually binding.

## Release evidence required for maturity claims

Use the following support labels literally:

- `structurally_intended`: architecture and canonical contracts exist;
- `fixture_tested`: deterministic fixture and expected-output tests pass;
- `profile_expert_reviewed`: a qualified domain expert approved the profile boundaries;
- `demo_evaluated`: blocking parser, leveling, conversation, and leverage evals pass on the reference demo;
- `pilot_validated`: at least three usage-cleared real packages passed with documented human corrections;
- `production_validated`: security, privacy, legal, operational, and monitoring controls are approved for a defined customer context.

Until the evidence exists, Tischlerarbeiten is `reference_demo_candidate`, not `demo_evaluated` or `production_validated`.
