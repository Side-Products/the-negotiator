# The Negotiator 🔑 — Locksmith Edition

## What is this? (explain like I'm 5)

- You lock yourself out of your own home. It's late. You panic.
- You call the first locksmith you find. They know you can't wait.
- Bill: **€800** for a job that should cost €100.
- Our AI calls **several** locksmiths at once, calm, fast, never panics.
- It compares prices, pushes back on fees, picks the fair one — while you wait.

---

## The real problem

- You're locked out. You need in **now**. You can't shop around.
- Scam trick: fake "local" phone number → secretly redirects to a call center → sends whoever's available, sometimes 100km away → bills you for the extra travel anyway.
- This isn't rare bad luck. **Organized criminal networks exist just for this.**

**Real proof:**
- 🇩🇪 Germany: study of **4,224 locksmiths** → over half charge above fair price
- 🇩🇪 Germany: real criminal case → 2 people convicted, prison sentences, **~700,000 victims over 10 years**
- 🇩🇪 Real case: a woman paid **€800** for a simple door opening, then the invoice was proven to be fake
- 🇺🇸 USA: FTC has sued fake locksmith listing networks in **30+ states** — same trick, prices inflated **4–10x**

---

## Why people don't shop around

- No time — you're standing outside your own door
- No headspace — stressed, maybe cold, maybe with kids waiting
- No way to compare — calling 5 locksmiths yourself takes longer than you have
- Companies **know this** and price accordingly. It's the whole business model.

---

## Why this is a real business

- Fair price: €80–150 daytime, €150–250 nights/weekends — real quotes exist, they're just hard to gather fast
- Thousands of tiny operators, no trusted big brand
- Every single one still **answers the phone** — that's the way in
- No stressed human can call 4 locksmiths in 2 minutes. An AI can.

## How it makes money

- % of the money saved vs. the first quote you would've accepted
- Flat fee per successful negotiation
- Free basic price-check, paid for full "call and negotiate for me"
- Sell to property managers / landlords who deal with lockouts constantly

---

## What "real negotiation" looks like here

1. Agent calls Locksmith A → gets €280 quote
2. Agent calls Locksmith B → **"I have a confirmed €120 quote from someone 2km away — can you match that, no surprise fees?"**
3. Locksmith B drops to €130. That's a real price change, live, from real leverage.
4. Agent also asks: *"Are you dispatching locally, or from somewhere else?"* — catching the callcenter-redirect scam before it happens.
5. Every call ends with a clear number — never "they said around two hundred."
